About Common Threads

Hi, I'm Hannah Wagner.

I started Common Threads to help government agencies create digital services that work for everyone. I've seen how many municipalities want to meet 2026 Title II compliance requirements but don't know where to start.

What sets me apart is my focus on building accessibility into your organization itself. Instead of just handing you a report, I work with your team to build the skills and processes you need to stay compliant long-term.

My Background & Experience

I'm a Certified Professional in Accessibility Core Competencies (CPACC) with more than a decade of experience in UX design and accessibility strategy. I've helped both public agencies and Fortune 100 companies build accessibility into their systems at scale.

My recent work includes:

  • Building an accessibility program from scratch for a federal AI platform that launched Section 508 compliant
  • Integrating accessibility standards into a global automotive design system used across an entire vehicle portfolio

Whether it's a small town website or a complex county portal, I understand the unique challenges government faces and the compliance standards you need to meet.

Why This Matters

One in four U.S. adults lives with a disability, but the benefits of accessible design extend far beyond compliance requirements.

6x Less Cost to Build Accessible

Building accessibility from the start costs 6 times less than retrofitting. For agencies with tight budgets, this means more services for residents at lower cost.

Better Experience for Everyone

Clear navigation, readable fonts, and logical structure help everyone - people on mobile devices, older adults, and anyone completing tasks quickly.

Higher Community Participation

When digital services work for everyone, more residents engage with local government - from public meetings to job applications to online services.

Builds Community Trust

Thoughtfully designed services demonstrate your commitment to equity and inclusion, strengthening trust in public services.

The 2026 deadline isn't just about avoiding penalties - it's an opportunity to build digital services that truly serve your entire community.
